Integration

Logi Analytics Integration

After acquiring Logi Analytics in 2021, insightsoftware launched a full-scale web integration, migrating over 2,000 pages and preserving significant organic traffic through a comprehensive redirect plan.

Web Operations Manager
January – April 2022
4-person internal team + 1 external agency

The Challenge

Logi Analytics — serving CTOs, Product Managers, and Developers — spoke to a completely different ICP from insightsoftware. In an effort to expand their offerings, insightsoftware needed to integrate Logi Analytics' product portfolio into insightsoftware.com while both preserving their organic traffic and authority in the industry as well as seamlessly blending the audiences in a single web experience.

The Approach

With such a difference in ICP, marrying these two audiences wasn't something we could do overnight. We dedicated a significant portion of the migration to pre-launch activities and brand unification. This afforded us time to build a comprehensive sitemap, redirect strategy, paid media integration, and preservation strategy for their vast BI Encyclopedia, an SEO goldmine.

Process

Five phases from audit to launch.

01

Sitemap & Digital Audit

Audited 2,000+ pages, mapped content types, and reviewed paid media spend and results. We held deep-dive sessions with the Logi team for full visibility.

02

Pre-Launch Activity

Launched brand-level paid media campaigns, paired with PR activities, to begin building awareness of the acquisition and brand unification plans.

03

Content Migration

Orchestrated a massive lift-and-shift of over 2,000 pieces of content, deciding what to keep, what to merge, and what to redirect.

04

Redirect and Go-Live

Built an extensive redirect map, linking Logi Analytics content to insightsoftware, and coordinating with IT to finalize the DNS changes.

05

Post-Cutover Stabilization

Launched a 6-month post-cutover campaign, using paid media as a mechanism to stabilize any organic traffic loss and begin CRO efforts for Logi products.

Results that moved the needle.

Migration

insightsoftware Web Migration

Managing a full-scale website migration from WordPress to a modern headless stack — keeping an 8-person team and two agency partners aligned across tight deadlines while building a new solutions-centric brand architecture from the ground up.

Web Operations Manager
March – September 2026
8-person internal team + 2 migration agencies

The Challenge

insightsoftware's WordPress corporate site had grown organically over years of acquisitions — 40+ products, a growing taxonomy, slow page loads, and years of legacy code. The site needed a complete overhaul, but the scope was massive: 3,000+ pages, multiple stakeholders with competing priorities, two external agencies to coordinate, and tight deadlines that left no room for miscommunication or missed handoffs.

On top of the technical complexity, we were simultaneously building new brand identities for each business segment — meaning every decision had downstream dependencies across content, design, and development.

My Approach

My focus was making sure this project kept moving and nothing fell through the cracks. With an 8-person internal team and two external agencies, the biggest risk wasn't the technical work — it was alignment. I established a clear cadence of standups, status reports, and stakeholder check-ins to keep everyone on the same page.

I managed our agency partners directly, translating internal priorities into clear briefs and deliverables. When bottlenecks came up — whether that was a design dependency, a content approval delay, or conflicting feedback from stakeholders — I stepped in to unblock and get things moving. I used Wrike to track every workstream and keep visibility high across all teams.

Process

Five phases, managed end to end.

01

Discovery & Kickoff

Coordinated the initial audit of 3,000+ pages across teams. Facilitated alignment sessions to define the new solutions-centric information architecture across three business segments: Finance, Equity, and Data & Analytics.

02

Agency Onboarding & Workstream Setup

Onboarded two migration agencies, established communication protocols, set up Wrike boards for tracking, and defined clear ownership and handoff points between internal and external teams.

03

Brand Alignment & Content Coordination

Managed the coordination of new brand identities being created for each segment — ensuring design, content, and development stayed aligned as each brand took shape. Ran regular check-ins to catch misalignment early.

04

Migration Execution & Bottleneck Removal

Kept the migration on track day to day — running standups, managing agency deliverables, escalating blockers, and ensuring deadlines were met across content migration, redirect mapping, and the technical build.

05

QA, Launch & Stabilization

Coordinated cross-browser testing and staged rollout by business segment. Managed the launch checklist across all teams and monitored post-launch for issues requiring quick turnaround.

Delivered on time, across every workstream.

Fundraising Campaign

Autism Awareness Night

Planned and executed a multi-channel fundraising campaign in partnership with the Pensacola Ice Flyers, raising $25,229 for Autism Pensacola to fund therapies, resources, and events for children and families affected by autism.

Digital Marketing Intern
January – March 2020
2 internal team members + 1 Ice Flyers marketing intern

The Challenge

Autism Pensacola was struggling with funding. The organization needed to raise money to sustain day-to-day operations and continue providing therapies, resources, and events for children and families affected by autism in the Pensacola community. With a small team and limited budget, we needed a creative approach that could generate both dollars and awareness at scale.

The Approach

We partnered with the Pensacola Ice Flyers to co-host an Autism Awareness Night — a live hockey game event that combined community entertainment with fundraising. I worked directly with the Ice Flyers' digital marketing team, meeting weekly at their office to coordinate our marketing efforts, plan event logistics, and line up prizes and special guests that would drive attendance and donations.

On the marketing side, I created flyers and social media content for Autism Pensacola's channels while the Ice Flyers promoted through theirs — giving us reach across both audiences. We also had custom shirts and swag made for the night and brought in vendors and hometown heroes to make it a memorable event.

Process

From planning to game night.

01

Partnership & Planning

Established the partnership with the Pensacola Ice Flyers and began weekly coordination meetings with their marketing intern. Defined the event concept, fundraising model (a dollar per ticket to Autism Pensacola), and promotional timeline.

02

Marketing & Promotion

Created flyers and social media posts for Autism Pensacola's channels. Coordinated cross-promotion with the Ice Flyers' marketing team. Built buzz around special guests, prizes, and the cause behind the event.

03

Event Production

Organized vendors, custom shirts and swag, and coordinated special appearances — including hometown hero Emmitt Smith, who signed Ice Flyers jerseys that were sold to raise additional funds.

04

Event Night

Hosted Autism Awareness Night at the Ice Flyers arena. Over 200 tickets were distributed to Autism Pensacola families, with strong community turnout beyond that. Multiple vendors participated and the energy in the arena was electric.

05

Follow-Up & Legacy

The event was covered by local news. The Ice Flyers continued their relationship with Autism Pensacola in subsequent seasons, turning a one-time campaign into a lasting community partnership.

Brand Redesign

insightsoftware Site Rebrand

Led the redesign and deployment of 99+ site pages following a complete brand overhaul — managing the CMS team, creating all page tasks, and coordinating across design and content to deliver a cohesive new digital presence that drove an 18% year-over-year conversion increase.

Web Operations Manager
March – December 2024
2 internal team members + 2 contractors

The Challenge

insightsoftware underwent a complete brand refresh, which meant the entire website needed to be redesigned to match the new visual identity. With 99+ pages spanning 8 different page types — product, product pillar, business unit, competitor, data source, solution, promo, and event pages — this was a massive undertaking. Resources were lean, so I wasn't just managing the project — I was hands-on in the CMS doing the work alongside my team.

My Approach

I received Figma designs for 8 distinct page templates and worked backward from there to build out the full scope. I created all the individual page tasks, assigned them across my CMS resource and two contractors, and set up a workflow to keep everything moving without bottlenecks.

Beyond task management, I coordinated directly with the design team to ensure new images and visual assets were created on schedule, and with the content team to produce updated copy that aligned with the new brand voice. When resources were tight, I jumped into the CMS myself to keep things on track.

Process

From new brand to live pages.

01

Scope & Task Creation

Received Figma designs for 8 page types and broke the full 99+ page redesign into individual tasks. Built out the project plan with clear assignments, deadlines, and dependencies.

02

Design & Content Coordination

Coordinated with the design team to produce new images and visual assets for each page type. Worked with the content team to create updated copy that matched the new brand voice and messaging.

03

CMS Build & Team Management

Managed 1 internal CMS resource and 2 contractors through the page-by-page build. Jumped in hands-on to build pages myself when the team needed extra capacity.

04

QA & Iteration

Reviewed every page for brand consistency, content accuracy, and technical quality. Worked through rounds of feedback with stakeholders to get each page type right before scaling across the full site.

05

Rollout & Optimization

Deployed pages in batches across the site, monitoring performance and iterating as needed. The full redesign was completed on schedule by December 2024.

Website Redesign

Sunrise Orthodontics

Managed a complete website redesign for a multi-location orthodontics practice in California — coordinating doctor interviews, copywriting, content uploads, media collection, DNS migration, and QA to deliver a site that truly reflected the practice's voice and values.

Digital Marketing & Partner Support Specialist
12 weeks
Myself, 1 copywriter, interns, + client team

The Challenge

Sunrise Orthodontics needed a complete website overhaul. The existing site didn't reflect the personality and values of the doctors and staff, and it needed to serve multiple locations across Elk Grove, Rancho Cordova, and Placerville, CA. The new site had to feel authentic to who they were — warm, professional, and patient-focused — while also being functional and modern.

My Approach

I started by interviewing the doctors directly to understand their voice, values, and what mattered most to them about how the practice was represented online. I then passed those notes to our copywriter so she could create website copy that genuinely aligned with the practice's identity — not generic dental marketing language.

From there, I coordinated across multiple workstreams: managing our interns to ensure content was uploaded correctly to the CMS, working with the Sunrise Orthodontics team to collect professional headshots, photos, and videos, handling the DNS migration, and running QA across the entire site to catch bugs and issues before launch.

Process

From interviews to launch in 12 weeks.

01

Discovery & Doctor Interviews

Conducted interviews with the doctors to capture their voice, values, and vision for how the practice should be presented online. Gathered notes on tone, key messaging, and what set Sunrise Orthodontics apart.

02

Copy & Content Direction

Passed interview notes to the copywriter with clear direction on voice and tone. Reviewed drafts to ensure the copy felt authentic to the doctors and staff rather than generic.

03

Media Collection & Content Uploads

Coordinated with the Sunrise Orthodontics team to receive professional headshots, photos, and videos. Managed interns to upload all content to the CMS accurately and on schedule.

04

DNS Migration & Technical Setup

Handled the DNS cutover to move the site to its new home, ensuring zero downtime and a clean transition from the old site.

05

QA & Launch

Ran a full quality assurance pass across the site — checking for bugs, broken links, mobile responsiveness, and content accuracy. Delivered the finished site on time.
